Expanding gender equity in sports beyond participation to include recovery, mental health, and long-term wellbeing.

Partner With Us

The Gap in Title IX

Title IX guarantees equal participation in sports. But participation alone doesn't ensure equal outcomes.

What Title IX Ensures

  • ✓ Equal opportunity to participate
  • ✓ Equitable scholarship distribution
  • ✓ Comparable facilities and equipment

What's Still Missing

  • ✗ Equal recovery support and injury care
  • ✗ Equitable mental health resources
  • ✗ Gender-specific wellness programming
  • ✗ Data-driven monitoring of athlete wellbeing

SportCARE Athlete Wellness Policy Framework

Three pillars for expanding equity beyond participation into the systems that actually determine athlete outcomes.

Recovery & Injury Support Systems

Structured protocols ensuring all athletes—regardless of gender—have equal access to injury prevention, treatment, and return-to-play resources.

Mental Health Training for Athletes

Proactive mental health education embedded into athletic programs, addressing anxiety, burnout, and the pressures unique to student-athletes.

Gender Equity in Wellness Resources

Ensuring female athletes receive the same quality and quantity of recovery tools, mental health support, and training resources as their male counterparts.

Real Impact

SportCARE doesn't just identify problems—it builds the systems to solve them.

Collects Athlete Data

Deploying the CARE survey to capture real experiences across recovery, training, and mental health.

Identifies Gaps

Analyzing data to reveal disparities in resource access, support quality, and wellness outcomes.

Implements Training

Delivering targeted workshops and tools that address identified inequities.

Proposes Policy Changes

Translating findings into actionable recommendations for school administrators and athletic departments.

Policy in Action

From school-level recommendations to systemic change.

School-Level Recommendations

Actionable reports tailored to each school's athletic department with specific improvement targets.

Pilot Programs

Structured implementations that prove impact before scaling, building evidence for broader adoption.

Long-Term Vision

Working toward national standards that include athlete wellness as a core component of Title IX compliance.
